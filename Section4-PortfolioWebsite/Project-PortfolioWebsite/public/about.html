About the Author

I'm a multilingual physicist and developer with a passion for building and understanding complex systems—whether through code, circuits, or theory. My journey spans from full-stack web development to low-level Assembly programming, with Python, MATLAB, and Mathematica as my daily companions. I'm just as comfortable writing backend APIs as I am simulating physical systems or automating lab setups. Electronics is more than a hobby for me; it's where abstract logic meets physical reality. I love designing and experimenting with custom circuits, bringing together the tangible and the theoretical in a way that always keeps me learning.

My background in Physics—particularly in Optoelectronics, Lasers, Nuclear Physics, and Elementary Particles—shapes the way I approach both problems and creativity. I thrive in environments where I'm surrounded by tools, components, equations, and ideas, often mid-experiment with a soldering iron in one hand and a notebook or terminal in the other. I'm not one to pose for the camera; I'd rather be captured deep in thought or mid-project, immersed in the process. For me, science, code, and hardware aren't separate interests—they're parts of the same system I've been exploring since I first fell in love with how the universe works.
